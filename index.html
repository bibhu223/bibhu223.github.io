About me

From magnetic materials to intelligent robotic perception

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Robotics and Mechatronics Engineering at the Daegu Gyeongbuk Institute of Science and Technology (DGIST), Republic of Korea, working in the Autonomy and Perceptual Robotics Lab under Prof. Giseop Kim.

My research combines magnetic sensing, LiDAR, inertial sensing, cameras, and artificial intelligence to improve robot localization and navigation where conventional sensing becomes unreliable.

I received my Ph.D. from the Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad. My doctoral work focused on magnetic thin films, exchange bias, planar Hall magnetoresistive sensors, and weak-field detection.

Research

Current research directions

01

Magnetic SLAM and Localization

Magnetic-field-aided localization, heading estimation, and SLAM for mobile robots in GPS-denied, repetitive, and visually degraded environments.

02

Multimodal Sensor Fusion

Fusion of magnetometer, IMU, LiDAR, camera, GNSS, and barometric measurements for robust state estimation and navigation.

03

Magnetic Sensor Technology

Planar Hall, AMR, exchange-biased, and harmonic-readout magnetic sensors for compact weak-field and robotic sensing applications.

04

Embodied and Spatial AI

Learning-based magnetic field modeling, multimodal representation learning, and physically grounded AI for robotic perception.
